A Daily walk can add years to your life

By Dr. Mercola If you want to add seven years to your life expectancy, separate 20 to 25 minutes for a daily walk. This simple habit, which can undoubtedly be one of the most pleasurable parts of your day, has proven to trigger an anti-aging process and could even help repair old DNA.
The research presented at the Congress of the European Society of Cardiology (ESC) monitored 69 people between 30 and 60 years of age. Those people who exercised moderately daily, such as a quick walk or jog, high-intensity interval training (HIIT), and strength training, experienced anti-aging benefits that could add three to seven years to their life.
The researchers recommended a daily walk of 20 minutes to obtain these benefits, but, although I agree that a daily walk is a phenomenal tool for health, I do not see it as a form of exercise.
